Early Signs of Sewer Problems<h2>
Sewer pipes are buried underground, usually about 4 to 6 feet deep in #State, which can make it hard to detect when something goes wrong. While it might seem difficult to pinpoint issues early, there are certain warning signs that can help you catch a problem before it gets worse. When you notice any of the following, it’s time to call a professional for a proper inspection to identify the real cause:<list>

A foul, sewer-like odor lingering around your property^^


Drains in your home not flowing as smoothly as they should^^


Frequent wastewater backups in toilets, kitchen sinks, or bathtubs^^


Soggy patches forming near your walls, yard, or the edges of your property^^


Unexpectedly lush or dry spots in your yard, which could mean sewage is leaking underground
